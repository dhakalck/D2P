üîü 10 Steps to Developing a Policy Brief

Essential

Purpose: Visual framework guiding participants from problem definition through economic evaluation to final recommendations.

The 10 Steps:

  1. Define the Problem
  2. Identify Modifiable Root Causes
  3. Determine Policy Options
  4. Map Implementation Steps
  5. Create Resource-Input Lists
  6. Add Cost Values
  7. Build Decision Tree
  8. Conduct Economic Evaluation
  9. Compare Economic Evaluation and Feasibility
  10. Select Best Option and Write Summary

‚úÖ Policy Brief Checklist

Essential

Purpose: Quality assurance tool for reviewing policy briefs. Used by mentors and participants to ensure briefs meet D2P standards.

Criteria Covered:

  • Title (short, attention-getting)
  • Key Messages (concise, compelling)
  • Problem Statement (clear, quantified, urgent)
  • Policy Options (evidence-based, well-analyzed)
  • Recommendations (clear action steps)
  • References (relevant, high-quality)
  • Design & Readability (clear, accessible, 2-4 pages)

Tips for Using D2P Resources

üí° For Facilitators

  • Review the Mentor Guide thoroughly before your first D2P
  • Practice module presentations multiple times
  • Customize case studies with local examples when possible
  • Use the Policy Brief Checklist consistently for quality reviews

üí° For Participants

  • Download all templates at the start of the program
  • Review the 10 Steps framework before each unit
  • Test Excel templates before your first group work session
  • Refer back to module summaries during the mentorship period

üí° For Program Coordinators

  • Create a shared Google Drive folder for your cohort
  • Adapt agendas and schedules to your local context
  • Translate key materials into local languages if needed
  • Archive your cohort's materials for future reference
