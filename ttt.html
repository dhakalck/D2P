D2P Train-the-Trainer (TTT)

Building Sustainable Local Capacity for D2P Implementation

Building a Sustainable Cadre of D2P Facilitators and Mentors

The D2P Train-the-Trainer (TTT) workshop prepares facilitators and mentors to lead D2P programs in their countries. Through intensive practice, peer learning, and expert coaching, participants gain the skills to facilitate training sessions, mentor project teams, and sustain D2P implementation locally.

TTT Workshop Curriculum

Format: 3-5 day intensive workshop, held 1-2 months before a D2P course

Delivery: In-person preferred; virtual option available

Pre-Workshop Preparation (2 weeks before)

Participants must complete:

  • Read D2P Mentor Guide in full
  • Review all D2P course modules (L00-L20)
  • Study the 10 Steps to Developing a Policy Brief
  • Review Policy Brief Checklist
  • Prepare assigned module for practice presentation

Workshop Schedule (5-Day Format)

Day 1: D2P Overview & Adult Learning

Morning:

  • Welcome and TTT objectives
  • D2P program overview and impact
  • Review of 10 Steps framework
  • D2P course structure and formats

Afternoon:

  • Adult learning principles
  • Effective facilitation techniques
  • Group dynamics and management
  • Practice: Facilitating small group discussions

Day 2: Unit 1 Modules & Mentorship

Morning:

  • Review Unit 1 modules (L00-L08)
  • Practice presentations: L01-L04
  • Peer feedback sessions

Afternoon:

  • Mentorship fundamentals
  • Providing constructive feedback
  • Practice: Reviewing fishbone diagrams
  • Practice: Giving feedback on problem statements

Day 3: Unit 2 & Economic Evaluation

Morning:

  • Review Unit 2 modules (L09-L13)
  • Decision tree modeling workshop
  • Practice presentations: L10-L12

Afternoon:

  • Mentoring economic evaluations
  • Common challenges in Unit 2
  • Practice: Reviewing decision trees
  • Practice: Troubleshooting Excel models

Day 4: Unit 3 & Remote Mentorship

Morning:

  • Review Unit 3 modules (L14-L20)
  • Practice presentations: L15-L17
  • Policy Brief Checklist deep dive

Afternoon:

  • Remote mentorship strategies
  • Using Zoom, email, WhatsApp effectively
  • Maintaining participant engagement
  • Practice: Virtual mentoring sessions

Day 5: Program Management & Practice

Morning:

  • Lead facilitator role and responsibilities
  • D2P program planning and logistics
  • Managing facilitator teams
  • Quality assurance and standards

Afternoon:

  • Final practice presentations
  • Troubleshooting common challenges
  • Action planning for next D2P course
  • Certificates and closing

3-Day Condensed Format

For experienced D2P graduates or when time is limited:

  • Day 1: D2P overview, adult learning, Unit 1 review
  • Day 2: Unit 2 review, economic evaluation mentorship, practice sessions
  • Day 3: Unit 3 review, remote mentorship, program management, action planning

TTT Teaching Methods

The TTT uses active, experiential learning approaches:

üé§ Practice Presentations

Each participant delivers 1-2 practice module presentations (10-15 min) and receives peer and facilitator feedback.

üë• Peer Feedback

Structured peer observation and feedback using rubrics for presentation skills, clarity, and engagement.

üé≠ Role-Playing

Simulate mentoring scenarios: handling difficult questions, giving critical feedback, managing team conflicts.

üí¨ Case Discussions

Analyze real D2P challenges from past cohorts and discuss mentor responses.

üõ†Ô∏è Hands-On Practice

Work through policy analysis tables, review fishbone diagrams, troubleshoot decision tree models.

üìù Reflection

Daily reflection sessions to consolidate learning and plan application strategies.

Who Should Attend TTT?

Ideal Candidates

  • D2P graduates who demonstrated strong performance and engagement
  • Health policy professionals with 3+ years experience
  • Epidemiologists/biostatisticians comfortable with data analysis
  • Health economists (especially for Unit 2 mentorship)
  • Academic faculty with teaching experience
  • Individuals with supervisor endorsement and time commitment

Selection Process

  1. Nomination: By D2P facilitators or supervisors
  2. Endorsement: Written support from senior leadership
  3. Application: Statement of interest and availability
  4. Interview: Discussion of motivation and commitment (if needed)
  5. Confirmation: 2-3 weeks before TTT

Prerequisites

  • Understanding of D2P program goals and methods
  • Strong written and verbal communication skills
  • Ability to commit to:
    • Pre-workshop preparation (10-15 hours)
    • Full attendance at TTT workshop
    • Co-mentoring next D2P cohort
  • Access to computer and internet for virtual components

After the TTT Workshop

Immediate Next Steps (1-2 months after TTT)

  • Assigned to co-mentor a D2P project group
  • Paired with experienced mentor for support
  • Receive module assignment for facilitation
  • Join facilitator team meetings and debriefs

First D2P Course (Co-Mentor Role)

  • Observe experienced mentor's approach
  • Co-facilitate 1-2 modules
  • Support group work and provide feedback
  • Participate in all facilitator meetings
  • Receive ongoing coaching from lead facilitator

Second D2P Course (Independent Mentor or Lead)

  • Option A: Lead a project group independently
  • Option B: Co-facilitate full units
  • Option C: Serve as lead facilitator (if ready)
  • Continue professional development through peer learning

Ongoing Development

  • Annual refresher workshops
  • Peer learning community
  • Access to updated materials and resources
  • Opportunities to mentor other facilitators
  • Potential to lead TTT workshops
