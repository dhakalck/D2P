Health Economics Train-the-Trainer

Building Capacity for Economic Evaluation in D2P Programs

Specialized Training for Unit 2 Facilitators and Mentors

The Health Economics Train-the-Trainer (HE TOT) is an advanced program for health economists, epidemiologists, and policy analysts who will facilitate and mentor D2P Unit 2 (Economic Evaluation). This intensive workshop builds both technical mastery of health economic evaluation methods and pedagogical skills for teaching these concepts to non-economists.

Why Health Economics TOT?

Unit 2 of D2P focuses on economic evaluation‚Äîthe most technically challenging component of the program. Teams must build decision tree models, conduct cost analyses, and perform cost-effectiveness evaluations. This requires specialized mentorship from health economists who can:

üéì Teach Complex Concepts Simply

Explain decision trees, ICERs, and sensitivity analysis to participants with limited economics background.

üõ†Ô∏è Provide Technical Support

Help teams build Excel models, troubleshoot calculations, and interpret results correctly.

üìä Ensure Methodological Rigor

Review analyses for accuracy, appropriate assumptions, and adherence to economic evaluation standards.

üí¨ Translate for Policymakers

Guide teams in presenting economic findings in ways decision-makers can understand and use.

Learning Objectives

By the end of the HE TOT workshop, participants will be able to:

Technical Mastery

  • Construct and validate decision tree models for health interventions
  • Conduct comprehensive cost analyses from societal and healthcare perspectives
  • Calculate and interpret incremental cost-effectiveness ratios (ICERs)
  • Perform one-way and multi-way sensitivity analyses
  • Apply discounting appropriately for costs and health outcomes
  • Use Excel proficiently for economic modeling

Teaching Skills

  • Explain economic concepts using plain language and analogies
  • Facilitate hands-on Excel modeling sessions
  • Diagnose and correct common modeling errors
  • Adapt teaching approaches for different learning styles
  • Use D2P case studies effectively (e.g., syphilis screening example)

Mentorship Competencies

  • Guide teams in framing appropriate economic evaluations
  • Provide constructive feedback on decision trees and models
  • Help teams identify and fill data gaps
  • Support stakeholder engagement for cost data collection
  • Review economic evaluation sections of policy briefs
  • Coach teams on presenting economic findings

Who Should Attend?

Ideal Candidates

  • Health economists with Master's or PhD
  • Epidemiologists with health economics training
  • Policy analysts with quantitative economics background
  • Academic faculty teaching health economics or HTA
  • MOH staff involved in economic evaluations or HTA

Prerequisites

  • Graduate degree in health economics, economics, epidemiology, or related field
  • Experience conducting economic evaluations
  • Proficiency in Excel (formulas, spreadsheet design)
  • Understanding of epidemiology and biostatistics
  • Teaching or mentoring experience (preferred)
  • Completed general D2P TTT or equivalent (preferred)

Time Commitment

  • Pre-workshop preparation: 15-20 hours
  • HE TOT workshop: 5-7 days full-time
  • Post-workshop practice: Co-mentor Unit 2 with experienced HE mentor
  • Ongoing: Mentor 2-3 D2P cohorts per year

HE TOT Workshop Curriculum (7-Day Format)

Day 1: Foundations of Economic Evaluation for Policy

  • Welcome and HE TOT objectives
  • Economic evaluation in health policymaking
  • Types of economic evaluation: CEA, CUA, CBA
  • D2P approach to economic evaluation
  • Review of Unit 2 structure and modules (L09-L13)
  • Teaching economics to non-economists: principles and strategies

Day 2: Decision Tree Modeling - Theory and Practice

  • L10 deep dive: Assessing Health Impact
  • Components of decision trees (chance nodes, decision nodes, probabilities)
  • Calculating expected values and rollback analysis
  • Syphilis screening case study walkthrough
  • Excel workshop: Building decision trees from scratch
  • Common errors and how to avoid them
  • Practice: Teaching decision trees to peers

Day 3: Cost Analysis and Data Sources

  • L11 deep dive: Cost Analysis
  • Cost perspectives: societal, healthcare system, patient
  • Types of costs: investment, operational, opportunity costs
  • Developing comprehensive cost inventories
  • Data sources for costing in low-resource settings
  • Discounting: theory and application
  • Practice: Reviewing and improving cost inventories

Day 4: Cost-Effectiveness Analysis

  • L12 deep dive: Economic Evaluation Methods
  • Calculating incremental costs and effects
  • ICERs: calculation and interpretation
  • Cost-effectiveness thresholds and decision rules
  • Dominance and extended dominance
  • Cost-effectiveness planes and acceptability curves
  • Excel workshop: Complete CEA from start to finish

Day 5: Sensitivity Analysis and Uncertainty

  • L13 deep dive: Sensitivity Analysis
  • One-way sensitivity analysis and tornado diagrams
  • Multi-way sensitivity analysis
  • Threshold analysis
  • Probabilistic sensitivity analysis (overview)
  • Presenting uncertainty to policymakers
  • Practice: Conducting and interpreting sensitivity analyses

Day 6: Mentoring and Troubleshooting

  • Common challenges in D2P Unit 2
  • Troubleshooting Excel model errors
  • Helping teams with data gaps
  • Reviewing team decision trees: practice sessions
  • Providing constructive feedback on economic analyses
  • Remote mentorship strategies for Unit 2
  • Case studies: Real D2P challenges and solutions

Day 7: Teaching Practice and Quality Assurance

  • Practice module presentations (L09-L13)
  • Peer feedback on teaching approach
  • Quality standards for economic evaluations in policy briefs
  • Reviewing policy brief economic sections (Policy Brief Checklist)
  • Integration with D2P Program: working with general facilitators
  • Action planning for next D2P Unit 2
  • Certificates and closing

5-Day Condensed Format (For Experienced Health Economists)

  • Day 1: D2P economic evaluation approach, teaching strategies
  • Day 2: Decision trees and health impact (L10)
  • Day 3: Cost analysis and CEA (L11-L12)
  • Day 4: Sensitivity analysis and troubleshooting (L13)
  • Day 5: Mentoring practice, teaching presentations, quality assurance

Post-Workshop: Co-Mentoring and Practice

After completing the HE TOT, new health economics mentors follow a structured development path:

1

First D2P Cohort: Co-Mentor with Experienced HE Mentor

  • Observe experienced mentor's approach during Unit 2
  • Co-facilitate 1-2 Unit 2 modules
  • Support 1-2 teams with building decision trees
  • Review team models under supervision
  • Participate in all economic evaluation reviews
2

Second D2P Cohort: Lead Unit 2 Mentor

  • Lead 1-2 project teams independently
  • Facilitate Unit 2 modules (L09-L13)
  • Provide weekly mentorship during model development
  • Review economic sections of policy briefs
  • Available for backup support from senior HE mentors
3

Third+ Cohort: Senior HE Mentor

  • Lead multiple teams per cohort
  • Support other HE mentors
  • Lead quality reviews of all economic evaluations
  • Contribute to HE TOT workshops as faculty
  • Customize Unit 2 for local context

Quality Assurance for Economic Evaluations

HE mentors ensure that all D2P economic evaluations meet quality standards:

Economic Evaluation Checklist

‚úì Framing (L09)

  • Clear research question
  • Appropriate comparators identified
  • Relevant perspective specified (societal, healthcare system)
  • Time horizon justified
  • Target population defined

‚úì Decision Tree Model (L10)

  • Logical structure appropriate for policy question
  • All relevant health outcomes included
  • Probabilities from credible sources
  • Expected values calculated correctly
  • Health impacts quantified appropriately

‚úì Cost Analysis (L11)

  • Comprehensive cost inventory
  • Investment and operational costs included
  • Unit costs from local sources when possible
  • Costs adjusted for inflation if needed
  • Discounting applied correctly (if multi-year)

‚úì Cost-Effectiveness Analysis (L12)

  • Incremental costs and effects calculated correctly
  • ICER interpreted appropriately
  • Results compared to relevant threshold
  • Dominance identified when present
  • Findings presented clearly

‚úì Sensitivity Analysis (L13)

  • Key uncertain parameters identified
  • Plausible ranges selected
  • One-way sensitivity analyses conducted
  • Tornado diagram or table presented
  • Robustness of conclusions discussed

Common Challenges and Solutions

HE mentors frequently encounter these challenges in D2P Unit 2:

üîß Challenge: Overly Complex Decision Trees

Solution: Guide teams to start simple. Focus on key decision points and major health outcomes. Can always add complexity later if needed and data available.

üìä Challenge: Limited Local Cost Data

Solution: Help teams identify alternative sources (regional data, micro-costing studies, expert opinion). Emphasize sensitivity analysis to test impact of cost assumptions.

üíª Challenge: Excel Model Errors

Solution: Use Excel templates to minimize formula errors. Teach formula auditing. Build in error checks. Have teams calculate by hand first, then replicate in Excel.

ü§î Challenge: Misinterpreting ICERs

Solution: Use plain language examples. Practice presenting to non-economists. Focus on "What does this mean for decision-makers?" Avoid jargon.

‚è∞ Challenge: Insufficient Time for Analysis

Solution: Set realistic scope during framing. Prioritize most policy-relevant analysis. Support data collection during mentorship period. Simplify where justified.

üìà Challenge: Data Gaps

Solution: Help teams identify proxy data sources. Use literature values with local adjustments. Emphasize transparency about data limitations. Show impact through sensitivity analysis.
