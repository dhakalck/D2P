Bridging the Gap Between Data and Policy

The Data to Policy (D2P) program, co-led by the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) and Vital Strategies as part of the Bloomberg Philanthropies Data for Health Initiative, creates a sustainable cadre of public health professionals able to develop evidence-informed policy recommendations.

How D2P Works

1

Unit 1: Problem & Policy Analysis

Duration: 2 weeks (or 5 virtual sessions)

Participants frame health problems, conduct root cause analysis, identify policy options, and perform stakeholder mapping.

  • Problem framing and definition
  • Root cause analysis (fishbone diagram)
  • Policy options identification
  • Stakeholder analysis
2

Unit 2: Economic Evaluation

Duration: 1 week (or 8 virtual sessions)

Teams develop decision tree models, conduct cost analyses, and perform cost-effectiveness evaluations of policy options.

  • Framing economic evaluations
  • Building decision tree models
  • Cost inventory development
  • Cost-effectiveness analysis
  • Sensitivity analysis
3

Mentorship Period

Duration: 8-10 weeks

Intensive one-on-one mentorship where participants research and develop their policy briefs with guidance from experienced D2P mentors.

  • Weekly mentor check-ins
  • Data collection and analysis
  • Stakeholder engagement
  • Economic evaluation completion
4

Unit 3: Advocacy & Communications

Duration: 1 week (or 4 virtual sessions)

Participants finalize policy briefs, develop presentations, and learn advocacy strategies for influencing policy change.

  • Effective writing for policy
  • Data visualization
  • Key messages and recommendations
  • Presentation and advocacy skills
5

Policy Forum & Implementation

Duration: Ongoing

Teams present completed policy briefs to government officials and stakeholders, working toward policy change and implementation.

  • Policy brief presentations
  • Stakeholder engagement
  • Policy advocacy
  • Implementation support
